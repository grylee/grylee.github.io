About Me

My research interests center on the evolutionary forces shaping genomic variation within and between species. I integrate evolutionary biology with epigenetics and chromosome biology to study the evolutionary dynamics of a substantial component of most genomes - transposable elements. By focusing on the significant epigenetic effects of transposable elements, I aim to develop a unifying model that accounts for the evolutionary causes and functional consequences of the wide variation of transposable elements across the tree of life. To comprehensively address my research questions, I combine global analyses (population genomics, functional genomics, theoretical modeling) with functional studies (experimental genetics, cell biology).
